# Discovery Questions Template

> **Comprehensive question set for AI project discovery sessions**

---

## How to Use This Template

**Before the meeting:**
- Review all sections
- Select relevant questions (don't ask all 100+)
- Customize for client's industry
- Prepare follow-up probes

**During the meeting:**
- Start broad, then go deep
- Listen more than talk
- Capture specific examples
- Note data sources and systems

**After the meeting:**
- Organize findings
- Identify patterns and themes
- Document gaps and assumptions
- Create use case summaries

## Section 1: Business Context

### Strategic Priorities
1. What are your top 3 business priorities this year?
2. How does AI fit into your overall strategy?
3. What competitive pressures are you facing?
4. What market opportunities do you see?
5. What's changing in your industry that affects you?

### Current State
6. What's working well in your current operations?
7. What's not working or needs improvement?
8. Where are the biggest bottlenecks?
9. What keeps you up at night?
10. If you could wave a magic wand, what would you fix first?

### Success Metrics
11. How do you measure business success today?
12. What KPIs matter most?
13. What metrics do you want to improve?
14. How are teams currently evaluated?
15. What data drives your decisions?

## Section 2: Process & Operations

### Current Workflows
16. Walk me through your [critical process]?
17. Who's involved at each step?
18. What tools/systems do you use?
19. Where do handoffs happen?
20. What takes the most time?

### Pain Points
21. What manual work is repetitive and time-consuming?
22. Where do errors occur most frequently?
23. What processes break down under load?
24. What requires constant supervision?

## Section 8: Follow-Up Deep Dives

### Technical Deep Dive (if applicable)
- Can you show me the current system/process?
- Can we look at sample data?
- Can you demonstrate the workflow?

### Business Deep Dive

## Question Framework Templates

### The "5 Whys" Technique
```
Problem: [Initial problem statement]
Why? [First reason]
Why? [Deeper reason]
Why? [Even deeper]
Why? [Root cause emerging]
Why? [Root cause confirmed]
```

### The "Tell Me About a Time When" Technique
```
"Tell me about a time when [situation]..."
"What happened?"
"What did you do?"
"What was the result?"
"What would you do differently?"
```

### The "Walk Me Through" Technique
```
"Walk me through your process for [task]..."
"What happens first?"
"Then what?"
"Who's involved?"
"What takes the longest?"
"Where do problems occur?"
```

## Interview Tips

**Building Rapport:**
- Start with easy, open-ended questions
- Show genuine interest
- Use their language and terminology
- Acknowledge their expertise
- Be empathetic to pain points

**Active Listening:**
- Take detailed notes
- Repeat back what you heard
- Ask clarifying questions
- Probe with "Tell me more about..."
- Notice what's NOT said

**Getting Specifics:**
- Ask for examples
- Request numbers/data when possible
- Get names of systems/tools
- Identify specific people/roles
- Capture exact quotes for important points

**Avoiding Pitfalls:**
- Don't lead the witness (avoid bias)
- Don't solve problems during discovery
- Don't commit to solutions too early
- Don't skip "dumb" questions
- Don't forget to ask "What else?"
